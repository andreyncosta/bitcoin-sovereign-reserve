# Bitcoin as Sovereign Store of Value

**Research repository — FGV EPPG Professional Master's in Economics**

This repository supports my master's thesis investigating the macroeconomic and institutional consequences of a world in which Bitcoin and the US dollar coexist as sovereign stores of value — not as competitors, but as parallel reserve assets with distinct properties.

---

## Research Question

> What are the macroeconomic and institutional implications of Bitcoin and the dollar simultaneously functioning as sovereign stores of value in the global monetary system?

The question is not whether Bitcoin *should* be a reserve asset — several nation-states and sovereign wealth funds are already treating it as one. The question is what happens to monetary policy transmission, fiscal space, exchange rate dynamics, and institutional design when they do.

## Research Scope

### Monetary Economics
- How does a dual-reserve regime affect money supply control and interest rate transmission?
- What happens to the Mundell-Fleming trilemma when one reserve asset is natively borderless?
- Seigniorage implications for dollar-dependent economies (especially in Latin America)

### Institutional Economics
- Sovereign adoption incentives: when does a nation-state choose BTC reserves over dollar reserves?
- IMF/World Bank governance under a dual-reserve system
- Constitutional and legal frameworks for sovereign BTC holdings

### Empirical Analysis
- Cross-country panel data on BTC reserve adoption signals
- Correlation analysis: BTC price dynamics vs. dollar index, gold, and EM currencies
- Instrumental variable estimation for adoption causal effects
